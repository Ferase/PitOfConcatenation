Terminology

General Terms

  • DAW

    An acronym for Digital Audio Workstation. DAWs are the tools musicians who digitally compose music use to actually create their works.

  • MIDI

    An acronym for Musical Instrument Digital Interface. A protocol developed in 1983 for digital musical instruments, such as keyboards and electronic drums, to communicate between each other or to other electronic devices.

  • VST

    An acronym for Virtual Studio Technology. Regarding music software only, VSTs are plugins, or add-ons, for a DAW that include more instruments, sounds, effects, or tools for the user to utilize.

  • Soundfont

    A library of sounds and samples comprised into usable instruments when loaded into a soundfont player inside of a DAW.
