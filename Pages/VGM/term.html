Terminology

General Terms

  • Chiptune

    Also sometimes referred to as 8-bit, chiptune is a genre of music that relates heavily to retro games. Songs in this genre are often composed using simple shape waveforms (square, sawtooth, triangle, etc.) similar to those produced by the Nintendo Entertainment System (NES) or other retro game consoles.

  • DAW

    An acronym for Digital Audio Workstation. DAWs are the tools musicians who digitally compose music use to actually create their works.

  • Decibel

    Often abbreviated as dB, decibels are used as a measurement of the amplitude of any given piece of audio.

  • Delay

    An audio effect that adds a repeating echo to any given audio. It can typically be used to mimic the rebounding effect of sound heard when making a loud sound in a large area.

  • Distortion

    If any piece of aduio becomes too loud that its amplitude raises past the point which the sotware, hardware, or both can handle, it becomes distorted. It can also be called peaking.

  • FL Studio

    A popular DAW that comes bundled with countless plugins, tools, and features for easily creating music. It has come to be a popular program within the VGM Analysis community for its variety of tools and its past history with popular VGM artists.

  • Formats

    All formats of audio are differnet in their own ways. MP3 is generally the best for the general listener, as it is the smallest in size and has decent quality.

  • MIDI

    An acronym for Musical Instrument Digital Interface. A protocol developed in 1983 for digital musical instruments, such as keyboards and electronic drums, to communicate between each other or to other electronic devices.

  • Mixing

    Refers to the process of balancing audio within a song so that the music flows more cohesively. This process can entail volume adjustment, effect application and manipulation, or anything else involving curation of all of the sounds and their relationships within a song.

  • Reverb

    An audio effect that adds a falling echo to any given audio. This can be used to make any audio sound as if it is being played in a large hall, in the distance, or within any wide open or echoy space.

  • Sample

    A piece of audio, such as a sound effect, instrument, or even a clip of another song or other audio media.

  • Sampler

    Refers to a program or hardware that utilizes samples as its primary form of sound output. Can also refer to a program or hardware capable of recording and manipulating samples.

  • Soundfont

    A library of sounds and samples comprised into usable instruments when loaded into a soundfont player inside of a DAW.

  • Synthesizer

    Refers to a program or hardware that generates its sounds using basic waveforms, modulation, and various effects.

  • Track

    A song is most often put together using patterns adn effects, and these are arranged into tracks. What each track contains and what instruments or items they point to is dependent on the musician's choice, though they are often organized per-instrument or sample. Can also colloquially refer to a song within a soundtrack.

  • VST

    An acronym for Virtual Studio Technology. Regarding music software only, VSTs are plugins, or add-ons, for a DAW that include more instruments, sounds, effects, or tools for the user to utilize.
